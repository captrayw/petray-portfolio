交友不慎

Bad Company

+
"神性和修行是冲突的" 阿努仕说道，把突突车停在家门口。我也一身疲惫，新德里闹市的活力让我应接不暇，油光满面的小贩和酷热的天气交织在一起，浓稠的就像是堂屋里散发出的劣质香烛。我跟着阿努仕进屋，把买来的鸡肉放在桌旁，一眼就瞥到了角落里的神龛和被蜡油熏黑的湿婆画像。 "为什么这么说？" 我问道。阿努仕从厨房里拿出一些香料，然后把它们放在一个钢盆里，示意我把桌上的鸡肉递给他。"神给了你这世做人的躯体，你要学会敬畏" 我把塑料袋提起来给他，他接过后把里面的碎鸡肉倾倒在钢盆里，开始把鸡肉佐料混合起来。我感到不解，如果敬畏自己存在的方式就是放纵欲望和情感，那为什么防止"糟糕的人类"的出现的规则似乎是反其道而行之？阿努仕按摩着鸡肉，油脂和香料和手指混合成一种物体。似乎钢盆之内的世界从来都是和谐一体，直到肚皮的干预才被迫分离。 "你在质疑你自己的情欲是否合乎道德" 阿努仕继续搓揉着鸡肉，头也没抬的说道。"但是你忘记了你还不是神。"香烛燃烧的太快，我甚至隐约看见湿婆画像的脸在轻轻颤动。我是可悲的一个追求者，此刻我对神性的追求居然在阿努仕面前显得如此虚伪，而阿努仕的话语却是有一种不可否定的真实。阿努仕处理好了鸡肉，他端起钢盆去了厨房。 新德里的夜来了。土地散发着白昼最后的的余温，天黑的泛蓝，只能看见电线杆的背影。几只麻雀站在上面，还能够勉强的辨认出来。我坐在门口的矮木凳上，只觉得整个世界都像我压来，像是要把什么东西从我身体里挤出去。我又想起神龛上摇曳的烛火，我突然感到一刹那的永恒，我被踏实的剥离，却又永远存在。 阿努仕端着盛鸡肉的盘子，拿了个凳子坐在我旁边。他递给我几块，我们默默的吃着。吃罢后蚊子袭来，阿努仕受不了回屋了。我坐到夜变得漆黑时回去时，发现画像上写着一句话："गांधी जी"我幡然醒悟了。那段文字的意思是：甘地万岁。
"Divinity and spiritual practice are in conflict," Anush said, parking the rickshaw at the front door. I was exhausted too — the energy of central New Delhi had overwhelmed me, the oily vendors and the scorching heat woven together, as thick as the cheap incense smoke drifting from the front room. I followed Anush inside, set the chicken we'd bought beside the table, and immediately caught sight of the altar in the corner and the portrait of Shiva blackened by candlewax. "Why do you say that?" I asked. Anush retrieved some spices from the kitchen, placed them in a steel bowl, and gestured for me to pass the chicken. "God gave you a human body for this lifetime. You must learn reverence." I handed him the bag. He tipped the ground chicken into the bowl, began working the meat and spices together. I couldn't understand it — if reverencing one's existence meant indulging desire and feeling, then why did the rules meant to prevent "bad human beings" from appearing seem to do the opposite? Anush massaged the chicken; grease, spice, and fingers merged into one substance. The world inside the steel bowl had always been harmonious and whole — it was only the stomach's intervention that forced things apart. "You're questioning whether your own desire is morally permissible," Anush continued kneading, not looking up. "But you've forgotten that you are not yet God." The incense burned too fast; I almost thought I could see the face on the Shiva portrait trembling slightly. In this moment before Anush, my pursuit of divinity felt so fraudulent — while Anush's words carried an undeniable truth. He finished with the chicken, lifted the bowl, and went to the kitchen. Night came to New Delhi. The earth released the last warmth of the day; the sky darkened to a blue-black, only silhouettes of utility poles visible. A few sparrows perched on the wires, barely distinguishable. I sat on the low wooden stool by the door, feeling as if the whole world were pressing down on me, trying to squeeze something out of my body. I thought again of the flickering candle on the altar, and suddenly felt a flash of eternity — I was solidly stripped away, yet somehow always present. Anush came out with a plate of chicken and pulled a stool beside me. We ate in silence. When the mosquitoes arrived, he went back inside. I sat until the night was completely dark, then rose — and noticed a line of text on the portrait: "गांधी जी." It came to me at once. Those words mean: Long live Gandhi.

Published April 9, 2021 · In memory of Anush and Foster

留不留？

Stay or Leave?

+
留不留？她给我一杯灵魂爵士，杯中的冰块雕刻的很细腻，恰到好处，也不喧宾夺主。我饮不下去，也不是时候。我知道冰块终将会融化，我也不着急。"听说你喜欢神秘？"灯光昏暗，她靠近我，我却看不到唇釉的痕迹。 留不留？听别人说过，女人才是上帝最满意的产物，直到这时，我才知道原来抚媚的确是有质感的。空气湿润而充实，许多味道，很配爵士。我不由得去想象她柴米油盐的样子，我憎恨我自己，但这是我的防御机制，也是我最后一点的自我意识。 留不留？我才发现我根本不认识她，也不知道为什么会落得这种境地。我为什么踏入这里，为什么接过了酒杯。冰快要化了。一直都是这样的，我告诉自己，一直都是这样的。于是我神魂颠倒，我放游自己在这一片飘渺的虚无中，我想我应该躺在床上的，也许明天还有课，也许应该设置闹钟。这个梦过于真实了，我太久没做过梦了。迷糊中我喝下了那杯爵士，融化后的水混着浓度刚好，温柔却不失醇厚。我想我摸到底了吧，生活的底，carpe diem 她应该就是那一位舞者吧。 留不留？我沉醉的观察着她的眼睛，我才发现它是蓝色的，我原以为是棕色的，我是否是醉了？还是现在才清醒？那没有侵犯性的洞察，噢，她全都知道。我便甜蜜的服从了。她牵起了久违的微笑，她全身上下都说着话，但我迷迷糊糊的只是像个三岁小孩般听着。 留不留？嘿，时间不在了。 我只看见酒保手里的抹布和酒杯保持在了一个均匀的受力点，风扇停止了转动，啤酒沫像是蜡做的般印在杯面，我竟有力气不看着她了。我走下凳子，从侧面观察她，她的形象变得空洞许多，左手拂起好似正要抚摸我，她身上的香气也凝固了，空气中飘着紫色的粉粒。我向前走着，木板想要吱吱呀呀的发出声音，但他们来不及，时间没有了，几人围着桌子吹嘘着，我看到唾沫星子都要喷到对方脸上。弹奏爵士的人还是一如既往的陶醉，似乎有没有时间对他们说并不重要。我走到门前，推开，这手感我竟然觉得熟悉。 我向前走着，不往回看，只希望这座酒馆不会因为我而消亡殆尽。
Stay or leave? She handed me a glass of soul jazz — the ice inside carved delicately, neither too much nor too little, never overpowering. I couldn't bring myself to drink it; the moment wasn't right. I knew the ice would eventually melt. I wasn't in any hurry. "I hear you like mystery?" The lights were dim. She leaned close, and I couldn't make out the trace of lip gloss. Stay or leave? I'd heard people say that women are God's most satisfying creation. Only now did I understand that seductiveness really does have texture. The air was humid and full, thick with many smells — perfectly suited to jazz. I couldn't help imagining what she'd look like in the middle of everyday life, and I despised myself for it; but this is my defense mechanism, my last remaining shred of self-awareness. Stay or leave? I suddenly realized I don't know her at all, and had no idea how I'd ended up in this situation. Why had I walked in here? Why had I taken the glass? The ice was almost gone. It's always like this, I told myself. It's always like this. And so I lost myself, let myself drift in this hazy, boundless nothing. I thought I should be in bed — maybe there's class tomorrow, maybe I should set an alarm. This dream was too real. It had been so long since I'd dreamed. Half-lost, I drank the jazz; the melted water blended to just the right concentration — gentle yet full-bodied. I thought I'd touched the bottom of life. Carpe diem. She must be that dancer. Stay or leave? I gazed, intoxicated, into her eyes, and found they were blue. I'd thought they were brown. Was I drunk? Or had I only now come to my senses? That inoffensive, all-knowing gaze — oh, she knew everything. I submitted to it sweetly. She coaxed forth a long-absent smile; her whole being was speaking, but I, dazed, could only listen like a three-year-old. Stay or leave? Hey — time is up. All I can see is the bartender's cloth and glass held at a perfect equilibrium; the ceiling fan has stopped; the beer foam is printed on the rim like it's made of wax. Somehow I have the strength to look away from her. I step down from the stool and observe her from the side — her image has grown considerably hollow; her left hand rises as if about to caress me; her fragrance has solidified; purple powder floats through the air. I walk forward. The floorboards want to creak beneath me, but they can't keep up — time is gone. A few people huddle around a table boasting; I can see their spittle flying toward each other's faces. The jazz musician plays on, lost in the music as ever — whether or not time exists seems of no consequence to him. I reach the door, push it open. The feel of it is somehow familiar. I walk forward. I don't look back. I only hope this bar will not cease to exist because of me.
—Poetry
